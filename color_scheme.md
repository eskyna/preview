# The "Earthy Elegance" Palette

Color NameHEX CodeRGBSuggested Use
Warm Alabaster#F9F6F0249, 246, 240Primary Background. A very light, airy beige that keeps the site looking clean and modern without the harshness of pure white.
Soft Sand#E3D5C8227, 213, 200Secondary Background. Great for separating sections, footer backgrounds, or subtle card backgrounds.
Muted Gold#C5A059197, 160, 89Primary Accent. Use this for primary buttons, important links, icons, and hover states. It provides that luxurious "gold" feel without being overly loud or yellow.
Warm Cocoa#8B5E34139, 94, 52Secondary Accent. Good for secondary buttons, borders, or graphical elements that need to stand out against the lighter backgrounds.
Espresso Brown#2C1E1644, 30, 22Typography & Deep Accents. Use this instead of stark black for your headings and body text. It creates a high-contrast, readable experience while staying true to the warm theme.

## Application Guide (How to use it)

### Header & Navigation

Keep the background Warm Alabaster (#F9F6F0) with Espresso Brown (#2C1E16) text. When users hover over the navigation links, they can change to Muted Gold (#C5A059). Call to Action (CTA) Buttons: For the buttons you really want people to click (like "Buy Now" or "Contact Us"), use the Muted Gold background with Espresso Brown or white text.

### Typography

Using #2C1E16 instead of pure #000000 is the secret to making a brown/beige theme feel cohesive. Pure black can clash with the warm undertones of your palette. Images: To make this palette really sing, try to use photos or illustrations that have warm color grading or sepia/golden-hour undertones.
